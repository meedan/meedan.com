What we offer

01
Virtual and in person events and conferences
Participation in virtual and in person learning events organized by the network. These are opportunities for partners to showcase their work and share strategies and best practices.
02
Structured learning opportunities
We design collaborative and time-bound projects that meet the needs of network members and provide opportunities to respond to global issues. We also recommend members for conferences, internships and training programs.
03
Our weekly Newsletter, The Checklist
The Checklist features the work of network members and provides them regular updates on misinformation news from around the world—open-source investigations, industry resources and event information.

Defending Media Freedom in 2021

World Press Freedom Day, observed on May 3, is a reminder to governments of the need to respect their commitments to press freedom. It’s also a day to extend support to journalists and newsrooms who are being attacked, restrained, arrested and silenced for speaking truth to power. For this event we were joined by panelists Devi Asmarani from Magdalene, Indonesia, Lara Bitar from The Public Source, Lebanon, Tania Montalvo from Animal Politico, Mexico and Asha Mwilu from Debunk Media, Kenya.
